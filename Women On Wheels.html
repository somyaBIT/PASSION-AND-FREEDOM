Women On Wheels: Riding the wheels of Social Change

Published by PFF Family on March 13, 2019

There is a strong history behind the role of bicycle in advancing women’s freedom, mobility, and position in society. #PFF repeated this history by organizing a WOW (Women On Wheels) ride which they consider as one of the most significant events of their journey. It holds a unique position for two reasons. First, the group never imagined the level of impact a simple activity like cycling can generate to bring a Social Change. Second, this event inspired the founder to evolve this cycling group Pedal for Fun into a Passion Platform which eventually grew as an NGO, Passion and Freedom Foundation. The founder says, “Running an NGO was my childhood dream, but I never imagined that the path to this dream will be realized through our small Cycling initiative.” The thought of organizing the WOW event came during a routine morning cycling ride shared between two friends and volunteers of #PFF Anil and Reshma who claim themselves to be Passionate Activists. They would generally talk about politics and social issues during their cycling regime. During one such ride, Reshma discussed her dream of designing a program which promotes Women Drivers and Gender Equality in the city. This way she wanted to help underprivileged women and women from the marginalized community get a respectable job as a driver who otherwise works as maids and cleaners. The only thing that was stopping Reshma to initiate this program was funding.

But who can stop Passionate people, who have already made up their minds? Both Anil and Reshma decided to raise fund by organizing a cycling ride only for Women. After all what else a cyclist could come up with while discussing such ideas during a bicycle ride. Reshma had already thought of the program name as Women On Wheels, and it sounded perfect for the event too. This was an AHA moment for both friends. They both believed that the universe had conspired to make these two long lost friends meet again through #PFF for this noble purpose. This realization was enough to start working on the nitty-gritty of the event that would help them to raise funds for the driving class fee for the first women driver under the program Women On Wheels.

The event was concluded with an inspiring speech by PFF Co-Founder, Sonal and a thanking note by Gulnaz who was the first aspiring women driver to be funded for the driving class under the project Women On Wheels. Piyush Dungerwal, a lead singer from Oxyrock band, was kind enough to be part of this event and play some beautiful songs on Women Empowerment at the end to show his support to WOW. Reshma currently runs an NGO named DriverBen through which she supplies Women Drivers to Private and Government Organizations promoting Women safety. She finds WOW as a momentous event in laying the foundation of her successful venture.
